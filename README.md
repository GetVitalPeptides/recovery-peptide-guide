# Recovery Peptide Guide

Welcome to the Recovery Peptide Guide by Get Vital.

This repository provides educational information about peptides that are commonly discussed within recovery, regeneration and tissue repair research.

Recovery peptides continue to receive growing attention within scientific literature because of their potential interaction with biological pathways involved in healing and regeneration.

---

## What Are Recovery Peptides?

Recovery peptides are compounds that researchers investigate because of their possible involvement in biological processes related to:

- Tissue regeneration
- Connective tissue research
- Muscle recovery
- Tendon and ligament research
- Cellular repair
- Angiogenesis
- Recovery following physical stress

Different peptides are studied for different mechanisms of action, making this an active field of scientific research.

---

## Common Recovery Peptides

### BPC-157

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is one of the most widely discussed recovery peptides.

Researchers commonly investigate BPC-157 in studies involving:

- Tendons
- Ligaments
- Muscles
- Connective tissue
- Gastrointestinal research

---

### TB-500

TB-500 is a synthetic peptide derived from thymosin beta-4.

Research has explored its potential role in:

- Cell migration
- Tissue regeneration
- Recovery pathways
- Connective tissue research

---

### Wolverine Stack

Many researchers study BPC-157 and TB-500 together because they are believed to act through different biological pathways.

Combination protocols have therefore become increasingly common within regenerative peptide research.
